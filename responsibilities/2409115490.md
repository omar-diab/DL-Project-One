# Project Role: Lead Architect & Technical Designer

## 1. Dataset Selection & Strategic Alignment
* **Curated the CIFAR-10 Benchmark:** I identified and selected the CIFAR-10 dataset as the optimal environment for this experiment. I chose it because its $32 \times 32$ RGB images provide sufficient complexity to challenge a Deep Feedforward Network while remaining computationally efficient for the extensive 50-epoch training cycles required for our ablation study.
* **Guideline Compliance:** I ensured the dataset met all academic requirements, providing a balanced 10-class distribution that allowed the team to clearly measure and report the performance impact of different regularization techniques.

## 2. Experimental Design & Model Architecture
* **MLP Algorithm Baseline:** I proposed using a **Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP)** as our baseline rather than a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN). This was a strategic decision to eliminate the "spatial inductive bias" of CNNs, allowing us to isolate and observe the "raw" effect of regularization on a standard deep-feedforward architecture.
* **Intentional Overparameterization:** I designed the model with three massive hidden layers `[1024, 512, 256]`, creating approximately **3.8 million trainable parameters**. I did this deliberately to create a "worst-case scenario" of high variance, which served as a perfect baseline to prove how effectively techniques like Dropout and L2 Regularization could "tame" an oversized model.

## 3. Data Engineering & Pipeline Management
* **Pipeline Implementation:** I established the `torchvision` data pipeline, including the normalization of pixel values to a $[0, 1]$ range to ensure stable gradient flow during the critical early stages of training.
* **Rigorous 3-Way Data Split:** I managed the partitioning of the 60,000 images to prevent "data leakage" and ensure scientific validity:
    * **Training Set (15,000):** Subsampled to increase task difficulty and make the model more prone to overfitting, effectively testing the regularizers' limits.
    * **Validation Set (5,000):** Reserved specifically for hyperparameter tuning and the implementation of our **Early Stopping** logic.
    * **Test Set (10,000):** Isolated as a "gold standard" to provide the final, unbiased accuracy metrics.

## 4. Technical Synthesis & Presentation Design

* **Constraint-Based Design:** I helped identiying th main points to fit the  **3-minute window**. This involved identifying and highlighting "High-Impact" metrics—specifically the **Generalization Gap** (Train vs. Val accuracy) and the **Robustness Drop**—ensuring critical findings reached the audience efficiently.
* **Technical Deliverable Management:** While I did not deliver the oral presentation, I helped making the slides, ensuring that the visual data provided maximum support for the team's speaking points.

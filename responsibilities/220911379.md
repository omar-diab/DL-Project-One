# 📌 Project Objective

This project focuses on understanding **how different training techniques affect model performance** in deep learning.

The goal is not just to build a model, but to:

- analyze overfitting,
- apply regularization techniques,
- integrate multiple methods into a unified system,
- and improve performance through optimization.

---

# 🧩 0. Full Combination Integration (All Techniques Together)

In addition to optimization, I was responsible for integrating all previously developed techniques into a **single unified model** before applying optimization.

Rather than evaluating techniques in isolation, I constructed a **full-combination (Full Combo) model**, where all methods are applied together in one training pipeline.

---

## 🔧 Integrated Techniques

The model combines:

- L1 Regularization (explicit sparsity constraint),
- L2 Regularization (weight decay),
- Dropout (stochastic regularization),
- Batch Normalization (architectural stabilization),
- Data Augmentation (input-level regularization),
- Adversarial Training (robustness enhancement),
- Early Stopping (implicit regularization).

---

## 🎯 Purpose of Full Combo

The goal of this stage was to:

- simulate a **real-world training pipeline**,  
- observe interactions between multiple techniques,  
- identify issues such as:
  - underfitting due to excessive regularization,
  - conflicts between techniques,
- establish a strong baseline before optimization.

---

## 📉 Observations Before Optimization

The full-combination model initially showed:

- Test Accuracy: ~46%
- Very small generalization gap (~0%)
- Clear signs of **underfitting**

This indicates that applying many regularization techniques together can **over-constrain the model**.

---

# ⚙️ 1. Controlled Experimental Setup

To ensure fair comparison, all experiments were conducted under identical conditions:

- Dataset: CIFAR-10
- Train: 15,000 samples  
- Validation: 5,000 samples  
- Test: 10,000 samples  
- Model: MLP (3072 → 1024 → 512 → 256 → 10)
- Activation: ReLU
- Loss: CrossEntropyLoss
- Fixed Seed: 42

This ensures that:
> Any improvement is due only to optimization.

---

# 🔄 2. Optimizer Comparison

I compared different optimizers:

- SGD  
- SGD + Momentum  
- AdamW  

## 🔍 Findings

- SGD: slow convergence  
- Momentum: improved stability  
- **AdamW: best performance and convergence**

---

# 📉 3. Learning Rate Study

Tested values:

- 1e-2  
- 1e-3  
- 1e-4  

## 🔍 Findings

- High LR → unstable  
- Low LR → slow  
- **Best LR: 1e-3**

---

# 📈 4. Learning Rate Scheduling

Schedulers tested:

- None  
- StepLR  
- CosineAnnealingLR  
- ReduceLROnPlateau  

## 🔍 Findings

- Constant LR: less efficient  
- StepLR: good  
- **CosineAnnealingLR: best performance**
- Plateau: less consistent  

---

# ⚡ 5. Fast Experiment Strategy

To reduce computation time:

## Stage 1: Fast Experiments
- 10 epochs
- Used for:
  - optimizer selection
  - LR tuning
  - scheduler comparison

## Stage 2: Full Training
- 50 epochs
- Applied only to best configuration

---

# 🚀 6. Final Optimized Model

Best configuration:

- Optimizer: **AdamW**
- Learning Rate: **0.001**
- Scheduler: **CosineAnnealingLR**

---

# 🧠 9. Key Insights

### 1. Too much regularization can hurt
Combining many techniques without tuning leads to **underfitting**.

### 2. Optimization is critical
Proper tuning of:
- optimizer
- learning rate
- scheduler  
can significantly improve performance.

### 3. Balance is key
Best results come from:
> balancing regularization and optimization

### 4. Adaptive methods work best
AdamW + scheduler gave the best results.

- observing underfitting,
- and resolving it through systematic optimization.
